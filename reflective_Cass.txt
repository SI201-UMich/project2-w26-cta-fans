I’m not sure if the current system is doing enough to keep short-term rental platforms in check. 
Right now, the system depends on third parties to keep an eye on Airbnb and make sure they’re following the 
rules, but Airbnb doesn’t want to share their numbers and doesn’t let anyone scrape their website. I think the 
city should think about making some stricter rules, especially ones that might discourage big short-term rental 
businesses such as AirBnB considering the impact they have on local rental economies in already housing-insufficient markets.

When it comes to web scraping, it’s really important to think about what consumers want and need. A lot of 
legal stuff about web scraping happens when companies are sued for collecting publicly available data to help 
people make better choices. Of course, companies are all about making money, so it’s important to have rules 
that protect consumers too.

Instead of collecting data on each person individually, let’s collect it in groups. 
Even if the data is public, we should always take out any information that could identify someone. 
Also, we should stop creating “influential” content that uses this kind of data.
