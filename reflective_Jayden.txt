1. I am doubtful that the current system provides adequate accountability around short-term rentals. The attempted measure in 2015 failed because it did not pass the vote. The current system requires third parties to monitor Airbnb's activity and keep them in check. However, Airbnb refuses to publish its statistics and prevents web scraping. I believe the city should introduce tighter regulations, especially ones that discourage large-scale short-term rental operations.

2. I think it is important to consider the consumer's right to choose and be informed when discussing web scraping. Most of the cases discussing the legality of web scraping involve companies that have been sued for aggregating publicly available data to help consumers make an informed decision. Corporations, understandably, are always looking to maximize profits. Therefore, it is necessary to have policies that protect consumers as well.

3. A) Data should be collected as an AGGREGATE, and not based on an individual basis. Peoples' identifiable information should always be removed regardless of whether or not the data is public, and generating "influential" content exploiting this type of data should be banned. B) An external, private company should not hold so much sensitive personal data, as it simply cannot be held responsible with it. Companies like Clearview already have a history with data breaches.
